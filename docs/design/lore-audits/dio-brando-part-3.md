# DIO — Stardust Crusaders Lore Audit

## Decision status

- **Continuity:** Original *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure* manga continuity.
- **Selected version:** High DIO during the final Cairo battle, after draining Joseph Joestar's blood and before DIO's defeat.
- **Primary canon window:** *Stardust Crusaders*, chapters 248–264, with earlier Part 3 material used only to confirm established abilities and limitations.
- **Rules v2 status:** Approved character-specific interpretation for the parallel Rules v2 roster. It does not alter the active Rules v1 character.

## Why this version

The current card set already combines The World, stopped-time knife pressure, vampiric feeding, and ROAD ROLLER DA!. Those elements converge only in the final DIO battle. Selecting High DIO removes an unsupported progression problem: ordinary blood may restore a vampire, but the manga specifically uses Joseph's Joestar blood to complete DIO's synchronization with Jonathan's body and push his stopped time beyond five seconds.

This version therefore starts fully synchronized. It does not pretend that blood stolen from any crossover opponent reproduces Joseph's unique canonical role.

| Topic | Primary canon reference | Design consequence |

## Supported abilities

### The World

The World is a close-range Stand with exceptional physical power, speed, stamina, and precision. Its attacks are direct Physical harm with the Stand source-system tag. It is not Magical damage and does not inherently bypass every defense.

### Time Stop

Rules v2 represents one conscious activation as a temporary combat-round state:

- Enemies cannot play new cards for the remainder of the current Combat Round.
- Cards already played continue to resolve.
- DIO's owned cards gain Follow-Up while the state is active.
- Selected cards automatically `Become` stopped-time versions between timing steps.
- The state ends at the Combat Round boundary or when explicitly consumed.
- DIO's defeat removes the state, but does not cancel projectiles or cards already committed to resolution.

The state is binary. The canonical nine seconds establish this version's superiority, but converting literal seconds into arbitrary per-card counters would create unsupported action accounting.

### Vampirism and regeneration

Blood feeding supports healing and a narrow once-per-game defeat replacement. It does not create `Blood Focus`, generic Energy discounts, Strength stacks, Haste stacks, or Time Stop duration increases.

### Knife pressure

DIO uses multiple knives to exhaust Jotaro's limited movement inside stopped time. The stopped-time knife card therefore increases hit pressure and becomes Fast, but it does not automatically Stun the target after time resumes.

### ROAD ROLLER DA!

The road roller is a high-output Physical Splash attack enabled by Time Stop. Its identity comes from overwhelming force and the stopped-time setup, not from Bleed scaling or arbitrary blood spending.

## Weaknesses and failure conditions

- Sunlight and Ripple remain existential vulnerabilities.
- Time Stop requires conscious activation and does not answer an effect that has already prevented DIO from acting.
- The World is close-range despite Time Stop's global temporal effect.
- Damage to The World transfers to DIO under normal Stand rules.
- DIO is arrogant, cruel, and prone to prolonging a winning position, but he is also analytical and adapts quickly when threatened.

## Crossover adjudications

- **Death Note eligibility:** Ineligible. The notebook's first rule applies to humans; selected DIO is an undead vampire, not a living human. His former humanity does not override his current species.
- **Stand interaction:** The `system-stand` tag identifies the source framework. It does not grant non-Stand users automatic perception, damage access, or resistance unless another rule says so.
- **Resurrection:** Vampiric regeneration is not Resurrection. If DIO is fully defeated and later resurrected by an external effect, normal Rules v2 restoration applies unless that effect states otherwise.

## Mandatory feel points

- DIO is dangerous before drawing Time Stop.
- Time Stop creates an oppressive action-advantage window.
- Knives and ROAD ROLLER DA! become more threatening inside that window.
- His vampirism helps him recover but does not become a generic cost engine.
- He retains clear, source-faithful weaknesses.

## Forbidden outcomes

- A low-power DIO who must farm generic blood before The World becomes threatening.
- Time Stop represented only as ordinary Stun.
- Time Stop deleting cards that were already played.
- Blood from arbitrary opponents reproducing Joseph Joestar's synchronization effect.
- Part 1 techniques silently imported into the Part 3 combat kit.
